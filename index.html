Education

As I mentioned briefly in the About Me, I am currently enrolled as a sophomore at Messiah University. I am currently majoring in Computer Science with a concentration in Software Development, and minoring in Spanish. Until enrolling in university, I had been homeschooled for all of elementary, middle, and high school. I participated in many extracurricular events at other events, such as band at a school nearby. After discovering my passion for computer programming, I asked my mom to incorporate programming classes in my schedule during high school. Being homeschooled was a great benefit to me during this time, because I got to choose exactly what classes I wanted to take so my courses were always interesting. In freshman year of 9th grade I took AP Computer Science Principles, and in 10th grade I took AP Computer Science A(programming in Java). I received 5s on both exams, which allowed me to get a jump start on the courses required for my major.

Again in 12th grade, homeschooling proved to be an advantage once again. I was able to complete 6 courses dual-enrolled at Messiah University as well as a community college. This helped me even further with my college studies because I had completed 30 credits of college classes before graduating from high school. I am currently set to graduate in 3 years, with the class of 2024. As for my minor in Spanish, which I have not really discussed much, I plan to study abroad soon to complete the required amount of credits. I took Spanish courses in high school, like many other students. During this time, I also began working in a job with many hispanic coworkers. I found that I was able to use what I was learning, and this sparked a passion in me, to pursue Spanish as a degree. I have already completed all of my required language courses, and all that is left are culture courses which I will complete at the University of Granada, Spain, during my time abroad.
